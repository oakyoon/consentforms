Vanderbilt University — Informed Consent Form

Title:                            Comparing Different Types of Perceptual Expertise

Principal Investigator:    Dr. Isabel Gauthier — Psychology Department

This research study, conducted at Vanderbilt University by Prof. Isabel Gauthier, is designed to help our understanding of how people recognize objects in their environment. The experiment consists of one HIT that may require from 5 to 20 min depending on the task (the information is provided in the HIT itself). You will first be asked to provide information about yourself (sex, age, race, native language, highest level of education completed)

The specific task may vary and could involve the following:

  1. Answering a short questionnaire about your ability and experience with a certain category of objects (e.g., cars, dogs, the faces of people of a particular race).
  2. Matching images or parts of images of faces or objects, or recognizing them from memory after having studied them.
  3. Choosing the “real” word for a specific domain among a set of words: e.g., which of the following is a real city? Paris, Todato, Jurpi
  4. Trying to imagine the face of a celebrity or an object, reporting on the vividness of your mental images.

Your participation is voluntary and you may discontinue participation at any time without penalty. Participation involves no more than minimal risk.

We may contact you at a later point to let you know of other HITs you can complete from our laboratory. You are under no obligation to complete any other HITs.

Confidentiality: The only identifying information kept based on your participation in this study will be your Amazon Mechanical Turk WorkerID serial number.

Compensation: If you satisfactorily complete the study, you will receive the amount listed in the HIT directory. Payments are made via Amazon's payment system.
